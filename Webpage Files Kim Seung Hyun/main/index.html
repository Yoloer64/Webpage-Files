Top 5 Games of 2022

2022 was the breeding ground of hundreds if not thousands of new and exciting titles. Unlike 2020 or 2021 where game companies showed us the true bottom of the barrel executive decisions of unfinished and not very enjoyable games, this year, we have a lot of fun games to play!

Here are the top 5 games of 2022.

Number 1: Elden Ring

Elden ring is an open world game in which you explore a medieval-fantasy like setting with your character and battle numerous enemies. Following the Dark Souls formula, Elden Ring is extremely punishing to newer players and doesn't come with a difficulty setting.

Despite its punishing boss battles and lack of modern game UI that many companies use such as a navbar, minimap, radial item wheel, and more, the game is a breath of fresh air in the extremely oversaturated open world genre where games lead players to specific points of interests without really allowing them to explore the world on their own. Though many newer players have complained about the lack of markers and navigation tools, the lack of such distractions allows players to traverse the well crafted world of Elden ring on their own and stumble into unique situations, accomplishing a true sense of adventure that not many other games can replicate.

Number 2: Lego Starwars: The Skywalker Saga

While many people may not have seen this coming, LEGO's new game has garnered a significant amount of interest as it triggers a lot of people's nostalgia for older LEGO games. Set in the StarWars universe, the player mainly follows the journey of Anakin Skywalker and his appearances in numerous movies. Though the game isn't fundamentally different from older games, it does extremely well in recreating a lot of iconic scenes from the movies.

Despite its lackluster appearance, the simple controls and unique LEGO themed jokes pans out really well for a lot of players. As it is a game meant for a more family-friendly audience, a lot of the violence and gore is replaced with simple bricks breaking and death really isn't a true death. However, this only adds to the charm of the game and perhaps surpasses the movie's rendition of Anakin's journey.

Number 3: Stanley Parable Ultra Deluxe Edition

Now, the original Stanley Parable was puzzling, yet impressive. The game is based around a simple plot of a man named Stanley escaping his job as the Narrator "narrates" stanley's journey. However, during the journey, the player is presented with a choice; follow the Narrator's script, or go off the beaten path. Both options are viable as they lead to a myriad of different paths, outcomes, and dialogue from the narrator. However, unlike the base game, Stanley Parable Ultra Deluxe adds more than double the content to the base game, with more places to explore, more endings to find, more jokes to hear from the narrator, and most importantly, more buckets to adventure with.

Stanley Parable Ultra Deluxe is a once in a decade type of game where a branching path of actions so numerous actually comes together really well and works towards a single narrative. A narrative experience like no other, and one simply can't describe the feeling of playing it with a single word. And of course, how could we not talk about the narrator? The narrator's soothing voice guides the player throughout the game, and both narrates Stanley's actions while striking a one-sided conversation with him. This combination really sells a unique dynamic that helps sell the game's narrative further, and makes the faceless, bodyless narrator a real lovable character.

Number 4: It takes two

It takes two is a 2 player CO-OP game that has an extremely interesting premise. Though split-screen CO-OP is pretty rare to see, especially with advanced networking allowing players all around the world to play with each other with minimal lag, it is still refreshing to see developers continue the tradition. It takes two is a mainly narrative-driven game, similar to "A Way Out" and "A Tale of Two Brothers" where players control two characters within a setting with similar, if not the same camera to look from. The story follows a couple who planned on getting a divorce being turned into dolls as per the wish of their child who doesn't want to see them fight or divorce. In order to change back, the couple must complete a series of puzzles that require the cooperation of both of them. While the story may be a bit simplistic, the character building, different puzzles, and absolutely stunning visuals make up for it. Each of the characters start out as dislikeable, but as the players continue the journey, the characters become lovable and even relatable by the end. The unique and challenging 2-player puzzles also make players use their heads a lot, and also require cooperation, bring not only the characters, but real people closer together as well. And how could I forget the absolutely cartoony and cute visuals? The cartoony yet realistic visuals really sell the atmosphere of both the characters and the background while the color palatte makes the game stand out and engaging.

Number 5: Surviving Mars

While Mars may seem like an extrmeley attractive prospect to live in, is it truly? Surviving Mars is a Real-Time Strategy survival game where the player creates a livable environment for the colonists to survive in while precariously balancing their needs and wants. The end goal is to create a self-sufficient habitable area where the colonists could live as they please, or at least try to survive until the next re-supply ship arrives. You first decide where to settle the city on a randomly-generated map with randomly generated terrain and resources, and build out your civilizations from there.

While the game may be a real-time strategy survival game, it also encorporates a lot of civilization building aspects as well. Not only do you have to balance consumable resources such as food, water, and air, you also have to manage other resources such as electricity, communications, and money in order to purchase things from Earth. Not only that, but you also have to balance each colonist's needs, lest you want a strike or something worse to happen that may jeopardize the colonist's survival. There are also random events that players will also have to deal with, some good, some bad, some catastrophic.
